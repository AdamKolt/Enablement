How I Build Enablement

I treat enablement as a system, not a series of training events. Sellers don’t fail because they lack content — they fail because the content, the methodology, the tools, and the manager cadence aren’t pulling in the same direction. My job is to build the operating layer that makes them coherent — and to measure it by what changes in the field.

Principle 01

Anchored to the Live Sales Motion

Education only works when it mirrors how sellers actually sell. Learning tracks, certifications, and assessments map directly to the stage gates, exit criteria, and discovery rhythms of the live sales methodology — not to a generic L&D framework. Sellers learn in the same shape they’re measured.

Principle 02

Powered by an AI-Native Content Engine

A CMS as the single source of truth. AI tools (CoPilot + Claude) generating discovery banks, objection libraries, and battle cards at scale. A RAG assistant on GPT-4o grounded in CRM, tariffs, and product documentation — answering reps and customers in the flow of work. Content velocity is no longer the bottleneck.

Principle 03

Measured by What Changes in the Field

Certification completion, ramp time, conversion rate, target attainment, content adoption — KPIs chosen because a manager can act on them the same week. Manager-led reinforcement turns the operating cadence into a coaching surface, so learning compounds rather than evaporates.

How a Typical Engagement Unfolds

Whether the starting point is a new product launch, a struggling channel, or a fragmented onboarding flow, the sequence is the same.

Step 01

Diagnose

Where’s the gap — content, methodology, tools, or manager cadence? Talk to sellers, managers, and customers. Look at the data, not the assumptions.

Step 02

Design

Role-based learning paths anchored to the live sales motion. Certification tracks tied to stage gates. Content architecture and ownership model defined upfront.

Step 03

Deploy

Blended delivery (cohorts, live, recorded, CMS), AI-assisted content production, and embedded manager-led reinforcement. Build adoption into the cadence, not on top of it.

Step 04

Measure

KPIs tied to revenue and operational accuracy — certification completion, ramp time, conversion, target attainment, content adoption. Iterate based on field feedback, not opinions.
